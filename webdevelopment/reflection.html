Reflection

To be honest, I think I got into the media world pretty late. For most of my life, I wanted to open a pizza food truck and eventually a brick and mortar restaurant. Getting into the media world wasn’t on my bingo sheet until I realized I couldn’t really continue making pizzas for my eczema. About this time in my life, I had watched Interstellar for the first time and I really just fell in love with the film. I thought I was fascinated by the science of it and astrophysics but I quickly realized what made me fall in love with it was the filmmaking and how beautiful the film itself was.

So, from there, I really took to putting myself behind a camera. The first experience I had was a photography course my first semester of college and then the next semester I took a production course, opening my first door to opportunity. It was this semester that I had made the documentary about my mother and it was the first video project that was completely mine. Not to mention, this was a project I had wanted to do since I had really started to give media and production and serious thought.

From that video to now, I have helped a professor of mine with a video series project he has been wanting to do for a while, and I finally have a way to give back to him. More or less, giving back is how I can say I reflect on my work best. I have never made or thought about a video for any glory to my own self. I have only ever wanted to bring the glory back to God and give thanks/praise to him. Through the gifts that He gave me, I have been able to find a calling that I just love and it also acts as a means to help and give back to others.

As of June 15, I have been doing an internship with Good News Productions International, where I have since been helping them make and distribute media that is shared globally so others can learn about Jesus! That alone makes me reflect on the short time that I have been in the field of media and how much I have been blessed with opportunities. Through any doubts or shortcomings I’ve had, I still have a God who is always looking out for me and providing me with opportunities I never could have thought possible.
